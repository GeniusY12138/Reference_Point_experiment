Stage Two

In this stage you will revisit each of the 40 situations from Stage One, one at a time. For each, you face the same lottery over Asset A and B payoffs but now must state your certainty equivalent (CE)—the guaranteed amount you regard as equally attractive to that lottery. We will use a BDM mechanism (Becker-DeGroot-Marschak). Under such mechanism, it’s optimal for you to truthfully report your real CE for the lottery. Click here for the exact reasoning.


A random price p will be drawn from a known distribution:

  • If p is greater than your CE: we buy back your lottery at this price, and you receive cash p in ten days.
  • If p is less than your CE: you keep the lottery and receive its realized asset payout in ten days.


Final Payment Procedure

After both parts are complete, the software will pick one of the 80 total situations at random:


  • If it selects a Stage One situation, you immediately receive the cash you allocated to “today” in that question, and in ten days you receive the payoff from the chosen asset.
  • If it selects a Stage Two question, the BDM rule applies: you either sell back the lottery at the random price p or keep it and receive its payoff in ten days, according to the rule above.


You will now answer comprehension questions before continuing.
